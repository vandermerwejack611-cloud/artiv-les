Why Inequality
Persists

By Simphiwe Mtetwa

The Calculus of Power

In the mid-1980s, Anglo-American developed scenario models for South Africa’s future. Business had done its arithmetic long before the negotiations began. They calculated what would need to be conceded to preserve the system.

"Slaves made a deal with their masters!"

Steven Friedman

The Renovation Metaphor

"I could afford R10,000 for house maintenance precisely because I had considerably more than R10,000. My willingness to spend rested on a quiet certainty: that doing so would not destabilise me."

This is how inequality was constructed—those who offered concessions ensured they would not threaten what they intended to keep.

Beyond the Tiles

The challenge now is not simply to do more within the existing framework but to question the framework itself. The renovation goes far deeper than the tiles.

Simphiwe Mtetwa
